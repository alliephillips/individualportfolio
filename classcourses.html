Class Courses

These are my current courses I am enrolled in for the Fall 2019 semester

SI 310: Information Environments and Work

The objective of this class is to develop critical thinking skills to engage in problem solving, specifically in inforamtion work environments. One will learn hhow to write concisely, but well, as well as learn important management terms.

SI 339: Web Development and Design

This course provides a hands-on approach to learning responsive, accessible front-end programming for Web Design. Topics covered include HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery, additional frameworks, and the POUR design principles of accessible design.

SI 315: Interpersonal and Psychological Implications of Social Media

This course provides students with a strong theoretical foundation for understanding: how existing and new social media platforms can shape interpersonal relationships' initiation, maintenance, and devleopment; and social media sites' implication for psychological and social human processes.

SI 335: Social Media in Organizations

This course will cover several areas related to social media and organizations: 1) undrestanding the challenges and benefits organizations face using social media for communication with external stakeholders; 2) how organizations use social media to fafcilitate internal communciation processes and achieve other goals; 3) how social media changes the ways people collaborate to accomplish organizational goals; and 4) reflections on broader (e.g. ethical, privacy) implications of some of the above.

SI 300: Career and Internship Studio

In this course, first year BSI students will have the time, structure, and support to engage in internship search strategies,career pathway exploration, self-assessments, and more to strengthen their professional development in theinformation field.
